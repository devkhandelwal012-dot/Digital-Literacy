Account & Password Safety
Use strong, unique passwords for every account
→ Combine uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols (e.g., Shiven@2026!).
→ Never reuse the same password across apps.
Enable 2-factor authentication (2FA)
→ Turn it on for email, banking, Instagram, and important apps.
-Social Media & Privacy
Limit what you share publicly
→ Avoid posting your phone number, address, college ID, or live location.
Verify friend/follow requests
→ Fake profiles often target students—only accept people you know.
-Device & Network Safety
Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive work
→ Don’t log into banking or UPI apps on café/hostel public networks.
Keep your devices updated
→ Install software updates and antivirus regularly to prevent malware.
-Financial & UPI Safety (Important)
Never approve unknown UPI collect requests
→ Receiving money does NOT require entering a PIN.
→ If someone asks for your UPI PIN to “send money,” it’s a scam.
Double-check QR codes before scanning
→ Fraudsters may replace QR codes in shops/hostels.
→ Confirm the name before paying.
Do not share OTPs, CVV, or bank details
→ Banks or apps will NEVER ask for these on calls/messages.
-Scams & Awareness
Be cautious of “urgent” messages or offers
→ Fake job offers, internship links, or “you won a prize” messages are common traps.
