Do’s  

- Do protect your personal- information – Use strong, unique passwords; enable two‑factor authentication; keep your location, phone number, and home address private.  
- Do think before you post- Ask yourself whether the content adds value, could offend someone, or might be misinterpreted later.  
- Do separate academic and personal accounts– Consider maintaining a professional/academic profile for coursework, networking, and clubs, and a private account for friends and hobbies.  
- Do cite sources and give credit – When sharing articles, memes, or other creators’ work, include proper attribution or a link to the original.  
- Do engage respectfully– Comment constructively, avoid flame‑wars, and report harassment or misinformation promptly.  



Don’ts  

- Don’t share confidential or copyrighted material– This includes lecture slides, exam questions, unpublished research, or any content you don’t have permission to distribute.  
- Don’t overshare personal struggles without boundaries– It’s fine to seek support, but avoid posting details that could jeopardize future employment or scholarships.  
- Don’t accept friend/connection requests from strangers– Verify the person’s identity before sharing personal updates or accepting private messages.  
- Don’t engage in cyberbullying or hateful speech– Even “joking” comments can have serious repercussions on campus and beyond.  
- Don’t ignore account security – Never reuse passwords across platforms, ignore software updates, or click on suspicious links; a compromised account can tarnish your reputation.
